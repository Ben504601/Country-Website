Gyeongbokgung Palace

The grandest Joseon dynasty palace, built in 1395.

Commissioned by King Taejo and restored after 1592 Imjin War, featuring the Geunjeongjeon Throne Hall and lotus-surrounded Hyangwonjeong Pavilion.

N Seoul Tower

Iconic observation tower offering panoramic views of Seoul's skyline.

Built in 1971 as South Korea's first general radio-wave tower. Its multiple observation decks, revolving restaurant, and famed "Locks of Love" terrace now draw over eight million visitors annually.

Bukchon Hanok Village

Historic neighborhood of 900+ traditional houses.

Preserved hanok area in Jongno, with time-slot visitor limits to protect local residents.

Jeju Island

Volcanic island and UNESCO Global Geopark.

Home to Hallasan volcano, lava tubes, Seongsan Ilchubong, and unique haenyeo diving traditions.

Haeundae Beach

Busan's most famous urban beach.

A 1.5 km stretch popular for summer festivals and nearby coastal temple views.

Bulguksa Temple

An ancient Buddhist temple in Gyeongju, designated a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Founded in 751 on Tohamsan in Gyeongju by Prime Minister Kim Daeseong. Inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1995, its celebrated Blue Cloud and White Cloud Bridges and gilt-bronze Buddha statues welcome over two million pilgrims and tourists each year.
