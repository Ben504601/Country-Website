Gyeongbokgung Palace

The grandest of Seoul's Five Grand Palaces, built in 1395 and rebuilt after the Imjin War.

The grandest of Seoul's Five Grand Palaces, originally constructed in 1395 during the Joseon Dynasty. It was destroyed during the Imjin War (1592-1598) and later reconstructed by Emperor Gojong in the 19th century. Today it features the Geunjeongjeon Throne Hall, and the National Palace Museum.
